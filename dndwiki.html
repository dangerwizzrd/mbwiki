Rollo Vrinn

Background

Born with his twin brother Jarric to Vila and Xalen Viziir of An'draath, Rollo had already started life as an outcast. In the matriarchal society of the drow, giving birth to a boy is bad luck, but to have twin boys is seen as a baleful curse cast upon your House. Vila was stripped of her social status within the Hel Knights, and Xalen was castrated and exiled from An’draath to avoid further “failures.” Needless to say, family life was tense for Rollo and Jarric, who were more often than not just left on their own while their parents wallowed in misery. As young boys, they often spent their free time scavenging for fungi to eat, as their parents could usually barely managed to eke out more than 1 full meal each day for the growing boys. One day Jarric led Rollo to a hidden away cavern, kept out of sight by a narrow entrance. Within the cavern was wall to wall luminous fungi, ripe for the picking. As Jarric began to climb up to begin picking, his foot set off a glyph, which started lighting up underneath all the fungi. Before Jarric could get down, it unleashed a blast that buried him, blasted the cavern open, and sent Rollo flying unconscious back into town. As small rumbles began to cascade into more massive tremors, long-hidden pathways opened up into the city, unleashing hordes of vicious monsters and mutants. The Hel Knights were able to defeat this seemingly random wave of attackers but at significant cost. The monstrosities had made their way into town and killed several villagers and guards. The blast had left Rollo nearly comatose for weeks, and by the time he woke up, his brother had been pronounced dead, and his parents had disowned him entirely. While he was out, the decision had been made to make him a ward of the Elder Sage Aldnis Corealia. Aldnis felt pity for the young Rollo and seemed to sense great power within him. This proved right when Rollo turned 13, and his shadow magic began to develop, at times quite explosively. This only served to further distance Rollo from his fellow citizens, who, at this point, seemed to feel nothing but scorn for the "Accursed Son." Life had been hard enough with his mother's neglect, and even then, he still had Jarric. Now, it seemed, Rollo was truly alone.

The next two centuries became an exercise in restraint as Rollo trained to center himself and contain his emotions while honing his control over his shadow magic. Aldnis helped guide the young Sorcerer’s training, and gave him a somewhat stable home, despite his own low status within the city. Growing up, Rollo also knew kindness from Tabitha, the daughter of the Archmistress. While aloof and cold to almost everyone else in town, Tabitha always seemed to reject the notion of Rollo’s outcast status. While the two weren’t bosom buddies by any means, Tabitha showed Rollo genuine respect that he otherwise would have never known from anyone else. Others, like her husband Boyzon, made a point to do more than just scorn, Rollo, instead actively tormenting him for many years as children. Boyzon’s mother had been killed in the surprise attack, and he had spent his life blaming Rollo for it. Boyzon’s favorite pass-time was lobbing rocks at Rollo as he walked by, which seemed to only get more significant as the years went on. One day, while particularly high on dust, Boyzon stalked Rollo through the streets, before finding and lobbing a massive stone directly at his head. Boyzon woke up three days later, terrified of Rollo, with a large scar running down his face between his eyes. Aldnis was the only other witness to what happened, and neither he nor Boyzon has ever said what happened. Rollo, for his part, does not remember anything from that day.

As word spread of Rollo’s dark powers, much of the direct harassment subsided. However, Rollo still felt the intense isolation of everyday life amongst people who either fear or scorn your very existence. For the next two centuries, Rollo’s life coasted along, studying magic while doing the grunt work of a Sage who acted more as government employee than a collector of eldritch knowledge. One day, Aldnis and Rollo detected a disturbance in an outer cavern, one populated by many of the monstrosities that had cause Rollo so much of this grief. The two mages used a scrying spell to see a group of seven strange surface-folk, fighting their way through these hordes of monsters. After sending Rollo out to help these strange men, he brought them back to the Sage’s Sanctum to recuperate and meet with Aldnis. This group, calling themselves the God Slayers, was a surly bunch alleging to have fallen underground after fighting a vampire. Claims of time travel and ancient gods of destruction were thrown around, but their main point was simple, they wanted to get home. Rollo and this group were sent into a sealed away network of caverns to find the necessary components to fire up a long-dormant gate to the surface. After a strange and ambling journey, they were able to gather the required parts to restart the gate, which opened up into the basement of the Royal Palace of Aesi’ir. Aldnis urged Rollo to leave An'draath for the surface, and see the world for himself. With nothing left for him in An'draath, Rollo took the advice and stepped over the threshold into a whole new world. After a wild encounter in the dungeons beneath Aesi’ir against a Void Beast, Rollo and company were teleported to the island metropolis Tanil’i. He spent the next year traveling with the God Slayers as they dealt with a series of complex personal issues. After helping Grak Greenfaith reclaim his original Half-Orc body, Rollo went to Redhill to see the legendary Herald’s Tree. When he arrived, he met two sailors, October Tengu, and Rook Sands, who soon became caught up with him in a were-tiger hunt. After saving the town from two villagers turned were-tigers, a local mystic provided Rollo with a prophecy:

Accursed Sons, cleft in twain,
lost, alone, a heart profane.
Now above your world below,
seeking a cure for your endless woe.
Secrets revealed, the two of hearts,
together again from worlds apart.
But when a black moon meets the ground,
the one you lost may now be found.

Having spent nearly three centuries of life steeped in disappointment, Rollo didn’t want to get his hopes up. However, he couldn’t help but see this as hope that Jarric might still be out there alive somewhere. Since then, Rollo has traveled up and down Uldura searching for his lost brother.

Rollo Vrinn

Appearance

Rollo is a tall thin Drow man, with not-quite shoulder length platinum hair and a dark aubergine complexion. Rollo has what some might call “resting b*tch face” and a dangerous side-eye. Since the accident that took his brother, the whites of Rollo’s eyes have turned dark black. Despite how odd this might make him look, Rollo is exceptionally handsome, even for a dark elf.

Rollo is quite sartorial, with a wardrobe full of self-made couture clothing. He most often wears a billowing poet’s shirt, high-waisted dark pants and low top boots. He wears a fair amount of fine and funky jewelry, with multiple necklaces and rings also of his own making. Draped across Rollo’s shoulders is a gorgeous glimmering robe of cobalt blue and purple silks, with a lining that looks like a night’s sky with gleaming four-point stars all along it. It has a tall collar up to Rollo’s jawline and a glimmering trim.

Tabitha Xilton

Background

Born in the Lost City of An'draath, Tabitha Mal’achi Xoffi (née Xilton) was born to Thalma and Darriq Xilton, two prominent members of Zovenia Mistral’s inner circle, herself the Matriarch of An’draath. Tabitha’s father worked as the Royal Physician, while her mother bore the title of Archmistress, otherwise known as the left hand of the Matriarch. Though Tabitha’s relationship with her mother was often tense, as fierce warriors of the Underdark, they had great respect for one another. Darriq took care of most of the child-rearing in the family and, to this day, loves the two women in his life more than life itself. While her father’s submissive stature in his and Thalma’s relationship always bothered her, she knew her mother did truly love Darriq in kind. Each trained her in their areas-of-expertise, so while Thalma taught Tabitha how to kill, it was Darriq who taught her how to heal. To most anyone in An’draath Tabitha was known as a prodigy, a mighty paladin likely to take her mother’s place as Archmistress one day. But despite the many privileges she enjoyed, life in the hellish prison of the Underdark still had its challenges for the young Lady.

The caverns of the Underdark teem with all kinds of mutants and monsters, and as such, most women have to join the military and help protect their people. Tabitha was trained in the ways of the warrior from a very young age, tutored by her domineering mother. Thalma knew the cutthroat reality that lay right outside the palace walls and did her damnedest to make her daughter prepared. Despite her languid, dancer-ly gait, Tabitha was a natural with a sword, and by age 42 had felled more monsters than any other girl her age. On her 86th birthday, Tabitha was inducted as a Hel Knight, sworn protectors of the Matriarch, and all her subjects. Though some took issue with her quick advancement, Tabitha was an exceptional fighter and was nearly as well versed in holy magic. She took command of her first unit at the age of 169, the youngest in the city’s history, before being chosen as the Matriarch’s Personal Guardian 60 years later. This position, a prestigious honor for any Hel Knight, was, in actuality, a mind-numbingly tedious job. Her new unit was confined mostly within the palace walls, which meant she was as well. None-the-less, Tabitha had grown up close to Matriarch Zovenia, whom she knew as her “Aunt Zovi.” She long missed her days of monster-slaying, despite the genuine honor it was to be entrusted with her beloved Matriarch’s life.

Despite living as an otherwise acceptable member of the noble class, Tabitha could not help but find small ways to rebel against it. Tabitha suffered no fools amongst the nobility of An’draath and was dutifully kind to those with little power of their own. Her cold exterior can intimidate many, but those who know her understand that beneath it all is a righteous and loving heart. Because Tabitha was born into the noble House Xilton, she was not allowed to socialize in regular circles as a child. For a long time, Tabitha had only one friend amongst the stuffy nobility, Lady Zivana “Ana” Mistral, the daughter of the Matriarch. Aside from Ana, the only other "real" friend she had growing up was Boyzon Xoffi, whose father would often bring him along on deliveries made to Tabitha’s father. His father was a lousy alchemist, but thankfully good enough to teach Boyzon, who was a prodigy at the magical art. His mother was a decorated soldier who had died in a mutant invasion caused by an incident involving Rollo Vrinn and his twin brother Jarric. Boyzon supplied Tabitha and Zivana with drugs like Dust, a depressant made of fermented bone meal, which helped keep him within their inner circle and close to Tabitha. The two dated for over 60 years, eventually deciding to marry and move out to The Webs. Before their ceremony, Tabitha gave Boyzon an ultimatum that if they were to stay together, he must stop terrorizing Rollo Vrinn like a childish bully. Boyzon had a long-running hatred of Rollo, due to his mother’s untimely death, but ultimately relented out of love for Tabitha. In a bucking of drow tradition and her greatest insubordination against her mother, Tabitha chose to take on Boyzon’s family name, rather than him taking the far-more-prestigious Xilton name.

One night after a family dinner with her parents, Thalma Xilton died in her sleep. All of An'draath fell into mourning after attempts to raise her failed. While searching for Thalma’s prized dagger Widowermaker, for her funeral, Tabitha returned home to find a sending stone in the belongings of Boyzon. In it were messages from a person saved in the stone as “Daddy” instructing Boyzon to kill Thalma, “collect a sample,” and steal the dagger. Boyzon was supposed to make contact with this “Daddy,” who would then reward him with a new life on the surface. Tabitha heard Boyzon return home and hid amongst the shadows, as he began to pack, looking for his Sending Stone. The only thing he found that night was Tabitha’s blade in his back, piercing his heart and killing him instantly. Tabitha quickly brought his body to her mother’s home on the palace grounds, burying him in an open plot in Thalma’s overgrown fungi garden. With only a few messages and a name to track this person down, Tabitha snuck into the Matriarch’s palace and through the Gate to the surface world. She left only a note for her father and Aunt Zovi with a brief (and incomplete) explanation of her absence. From that day forward, Tabitha now had only one mission in life: to find & kill Daddy.

Preston Caviar

Background

Shawn Gilman was born to Mike and Terry Gilman, the second child in a large family of Merfolk in the underwater city of Icthydelphia. He grew up the oldest boy of ten children, and as such, frequently felt overlooked by his family. Shawn was a handful from a young age, often getting into trouble with older boys and other ne’er-do-wells across Fishtown. This behavior greatly strained his relationship with parents, who he felt resented his existence. Shawn also struggled in his classes and eventually dropped out two years before graduation. He felt he had learned everything school had to offer him and, after a particularly physical fight with his father, was kicked out of his parents’ home. Despite this tumultuous-ness, most of his siblings loved him dearly, knowing him as a kind hearted and goofy older brother. His younger brother Patrick always particularly admired Shawn, and continued to try and see Shawn long after he moved out.

Now on the streets, Shawn learned quickly that he would need more than just his affinity for petty crime to survive on his own. While he wasn’t born rich, he had at least been born pretty and soon found ways to lean into it. Life as a street hustler brought much excitement and trouble for the young mer, whose good looks still were not enough to keep him out of handcuffs. Realizing that small cons and pick-pocketing were getting too risky, Shawn soon switched to a “steadier” form of hustling as a male prostitute. Shawn lived like this for years, eventually working his way up to regular gigolo work for the old, rich and lonely.

Two years before the start of his surface-world adventures, Shawn’s life changed forever when he met Langston and Luther Cod, two investment brokers and old money socialites at a party for Icthydelphia’s elite. Langston took an immediate liking to him and his strange, affable charm. The brothers took the formerly kept-man and gave him a job, salary, a penthouse suite, and all the other luxuries money can buy. Most importantly they gave him a new identity and a new lease on life as Preston Caviar. 6 months ago, the Cod Brothers went suddenly missing without a trace. With the police closing in on Preston as their prime suspect he made a hasty escape into an abandoned church on the north side of Icthy. With no where left to go, Preston fell to his knees and began to pray. He didn’t believe a word of it, but somehow still received an answer, in the form of powerful arcane abilities. These powers helped him evade capture and exit the city via an abandoned bathysphere on the outskirts of town, shown to him in visions.

As he slept during his hours long journey to the surface, Preston had strange dreams of an Aurelia, Matron of the Seas, the powerful Archfey who’s dilapidated temple he had hidden in. She told Preston only to find her. Since then Preston has traveled the surface in hopes of finding the Cod Brothers with his friends October and Tabitha. He also recently tried drug dealing for the first time.

Suplex Hammerthorn

Background

Suplex was born as Drenk Nuut, raised as a scavenger in the Drowning Mire swamps by the Chief Scrounger, Urtejj Nuut. Like most goblins of his size, Suplex was used to squeeze into all manner of horrifying situations to gather materials for his clan. Eventually, he became skilled at slinking and sliding through dangerous situations, and gave up scavenging for life as a cutpurse in the central swamp cities of Ogzos. It was there he first met his mentor Zolda Yerrt, a fellow pack runt and an old witch. She hired Drenk to steal some of the more expensive components for her, and in exchange, taught him some rudimentary magic.

The two enjoyed this mutually beneficial relationship for quite some time, until Urtejj came to town, having finally tracked Drenk down after a long and arduous search. Urtejj and his boys gave Drenk a public thrashing before Zolda found out and intervened. Zolda claimed Drenk as her apprentice seer, and in doing so, outranked Urtejj. With him now officially her apprentice, Zolda began to take his arcane studies more seriously. She taught him the ways of divination and how to read and manipulate portents of fate.

It didn’t take long for things to go wrong, when soon after that, Zolda gave her Warchief an unfavorable prediction and was set for execution. Slipping through the gang forming around the bugbears that had bound and dragged Zolda through the streets, Drenk created a magical distraction of himself shitting in public. This distracted the dull-minded bugbears long enough for the real Drenk to slip in and free Zolda. The two were chased for hours into the swamplands, only to finally lose their trackers as night fell.

The next morning the two goblins continue slowly out into the deserts outside of Astramorn. Magically disguising themselves as Gnomes, they hitched a ride with a nearby caravan. The two slipped through the border into the City of Mages, where they immediately took to the slums, settling in an abandoned water tower. For the next few years, the two lived in relative peace, with Drenk taking on the name of Suplex Hammerthorn and disguising themselves when out and about. Suplex supported them through good ol’ fashion thievery. At the same time, Zolda made money as a fortune-teller known as the “Gran Swami,” who became popular amongst the children of Motown. For the first time in his existence, Suplex had a relatively normal life, and something kind of like a family. He was happy. It didn’t last.

One night, after pickpocketing all day long, Suplex went to get Zolda and walk home from her Gran Swami gig. As their magical disguises began to fade, they began ducking through alleyways. Rounding a corner, they were stopped by a man mugging a woman who stabbed her while trying to grab her purse. The woman still fought with her life to hold onto her bag, as Suplex and Zolda blasted the thief with magic, causing him to run. Suplex ran to the woman, who was quickly bleeding out, as a few citizens ran over to see what the commotion was about. In her final act, the woman handed her bag to Suplex, begging him to run. The humans who stumbled upon them began to accuse the goblins of the mugging and called for help. Echoing the now dead woman’s sentiments, Zolda told Suplex to run, as guards arrived and began to take her into custody. Darting through the alleyways, Suplex managed to avoid capture, but he could feel the heat closing in on him. Back at their water tank hide-away, Suplex went through the purse, finding an airship ticket to Breakwater, a wallet, and a necklace. The necklace was strange, with a solid crystal pendant like a four-point star with clockwork gears around it. A few hours later, Zolda managed to send one last magical message to him before she was imprisoned.

“Get outta town and lay low for a while, kid! But please, don’t forget about me.”
“I’m fuckin’ serious!”

Unsure of what lies before him, Suplex took a few trinkets and what he had on him and made his way to the docks. Forging his assumed name onto the ticket, he boarded a ship and left for Uldura, the Savage County.

Zolda Yerrt

Background

Zolda Yerrt was born on a humid day in 1345 a.d. to Zorrek Yerrt and a hobgoblin mother from a breeding den as the runt of her litter. Zolda was gifted with foresight from a young age, and was trained as a Seer by the elder witches in her tribe. Eventually she made enough of a name for herself to be moved from the outer reaches of the swamps to the city of Ogzos, where she was employed by Warchiefs to portend unseen attacks from the many hostile states surrounding the swamp.

Zolda built as good a reputation as can be expected in Goblin society, and was well known around the city for her prowess in the magical art of Divination. Even still, she was often the bearer of bad news to the many Warchiefs who would call on her services. Because of this she was hardly popular amongst the elite, and a few times even had to go into hiding until those Warchiefs chilled the "F" out.

Soon Zolda met Suplex, a local thief, and tasked him with acquiring expensive and rare components for use in her spell casting. As payment she would teach him basic magic, which the young goblin took to quickly. Despite his often outlandish and brash behavior, Zolda saw great potential in the boy. She took him under her wing as an apprentice, and began to teach him as a fully fledged wizard.

Eventually, Zolda's luck with the Warchiefs ran out for good, and after a particularly unsavory prediction for Warchief Zuul Garjevv, she found herself being dragged out into public to be executed. Using his newly learned magic, Suplex managed to distract her captors with a rather grisly fecal illusion, and dove into danger in order to gran her and run. It took over a week for them to successfully sneak through the Drowning Mire unseen, but soon the pair had managed to escape and sneak through the border into Astramorn.

Living with Suplex in the magical city of Astramorn, the two became even closer, relying on each other to survive in a place hostile to Goblins. Zolda masqueraded as a gnome street magician while Suplex snuck through the streets pick-pocketing. Zolda, normally only concerned with her own well being, unexpectedly began to feel a motherly affection for her young apprentice. The two lived together in an abandoned water tank outside of Motown in the lower circle, managed a few years together as a weird make-shift family. Unfortunately it was never meant to last. The two were separated during a mugging gone bad that they tried to stop. While Suplex got away, Zolda was taken in and arrested for the crime that she did not commit.
